Lasagna

Description

Lasagna, also known by the Italian plural lasagne, is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made in very wide, flat sheets. In Italian cuisine it is made of stacked layers of pasta alternating with fillings such as ragù, béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses, and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 3 cups marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  4. Add marinara sauce to the skillet and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  6. Spread a layer of meat sauce in a baking dish. Layer noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella cheese, and meat sauce. Repeat layers.
  7. Top with remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Bake for 30-40 minutes until bubbly and golden brown.
  9. Let it cool for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil.
